MATH 6122 Algebra II (Winter 2025)

Overview

Introduction to category theory, additional ring and module theory, introduction to algebraic geometry, field theory and Galois theorems.

Prerequisites

GS/MATH 6121 3.00 or permission of the Instructor.

Textbook/Resources

  • Course notes
  • Algebra, by Thomas W. Hungerford

Tentative Material

  1. Fields and Galois Theory (Chapter 5, 6)
  2. Ring Theory (Chapter 3, 8, 9)
  3. Linear Algebra (Chapter 7)
  4. Module Theory (Chapter 4, 8)
  5. Categories (Chapter 10)

Format

Starting Tuesday, January 7, in-person lectures will be given at 1:00 p.m.-2:30 p.m. every Tuesday and Thursday at SC 211.

Assignments and Score

Homework Assignments (15% of Total Score)

During the semester, there will be homework assignments posted on eClass. A new assignment will be posted on some weeks, and it will be due after two weeks.

In-Class Participation (15% of Total Score)

Students should come to class prepared to present solutions to homework problems. The participation evaluation will be based in part on these presentation.

Midterm Exam (20% of total score)

A midterm exam will be given on Thursday, February 27, 2025. It will be a written exam given during regular class time and location that can only be written in person.

Final Exam (50% of total score)

A cumulative final exam will be given during the final examination period. The exact time and location will be announced later. It will be a three-hour written exam that can only be taken place in person.
